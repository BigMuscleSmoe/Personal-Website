Radiant works differently from every other rank in Valorant. There is no points threshold you can grind past. It is a regional leaderboard hard-capped at roughly 500 players. To become Radiant, you have to displace someone who is already there, and the moment you stop winning, someone displaces you. Out of millions of ranked players per region, that puts Radiant at about 0.03% of the player base, and #460 means holding a numbered seat among them.

sources: Riot Games: Immortal & Radiant ranking · Esports Tales rank distribution

// What it takes

Aim is just the entry fee

Everyone at this level has near-perfect mechanics: flicks, crosshair placement, and recoil control are assumed. What separates a Radiant player is everything that happens between the gunfights:

Decision-making at speed
Round economy, rotation timing, and utility usage are recalculated every few seconds against opponents doing the same. Wrong reads get punished instantly; there is no margin at the top of the ladder.
Composure under pressure
Clutch rounds at Radiant are played against the best players in the region, with a leaderboard position on the line every single match. Staying calm and executing is the skill.
Consistency, not a hot streak
Because the leaderboard is competitive, the rank decays the moment performance dips. Holding seat #460 means performing at that level day after day, not peaking once.
Deliberate practice
Climbing from Iron-tier matchmaking to a numbered Radiant seat is thousands of hours of VOD review, aim training, and meta study, the same discipline as mastering any hard craft.
